# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The team at TIST (a boutique, content-first influencer-marketing agency) using **Loop**, an internal task and project manager. Three roles:
- **Members** — do the work: create tasks, move them through `todo → doing`, submit finished work for review.
- **Team managers** — approve their team's work (sign off `in_review → done`, or send it back with a note).
- **Superadmins** — company-wide reach: every team/project/task, plus the workspace roster and the manager/superadmin assignments.

Context: focused work sessions, daily task triage and review. The tool should disappear into the task.

## Product Purpose

A calm, fast place to capture, organize, and finish work, with a lightweight approval step so managers sign off team output without ceremony. Success = clearing your queue (and your approvals) feels quick and obvious; the structure (teams, projects, review gate) never gets in the way.

## Brand Personality

Premium, fresh, precise, calm. Linear/Things-like restraint, not enterprise density. Confident and quiet: color and motion carry meaning, never decoration. Three words: **crafted, calm, exact.**

## Anti-references

Enterprise admin-panel drabness (Jira settings, generic Bootstrap CRUD). Over-decorated dashboards (gradient hero metrics, neon). Playful/cartoonish productivity apps. Loud, badge-heavy notification spam.

## Design Principles

- **State you can read at a glance.** A task's status (in review, needs approval, done) is legible from its row alone, via one consistent semantic color vocabulary (blue = primary/selected, amber = pending/needs-attention, emerald = done/success, rose = destructive/overdue).
- **Approval is lightweight, not bureaucratic.** Submitting and approving are one-click; sending back is one note. No multi-field forms for a yes/no decision.
- **Earned familiarity.** Reuse the app's existing components, spacing scale, shadows, and motion. New surfaces should feel like they were always there.
- **Quiet authority.** Power (superadmin, manager) is surfaced calmly, never with alarmist styling.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large/bold ≥3:1; semantic colors paired with text/icon, never color alone (e.g. "In review" + paper-plane glyph). Visible keyboard focus (`focus-ring`). All motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`. Light + dark themes both first-class.
